The King of the Fourth

An Interactive Feature on Isaiah Thomas

By Adam MacDonald

Isaiah Thomas has been a revelation this season. Despite standing at just 5'9'', Thomas has carried the Boston Celtics nightly scoring burden — single-handedly helping an oft-injured, star-deficient team steal the top seed in the Eastern Conference.

Oh, and he also happened to toss up one of the greatest advanced-metrics seasons of all-time. Not bad for someone who's the same height as my mom.

The Numbers Don't Lie

There's a reason Thomas has been anointed King of the Fourth by his beantown brethren. He's 2nd in the league in fourth-quarter points per game, only trailing Russell Westbrook, but scoring with superior efficiency.

In fact, Thomas is having one of the most efficient high-volume scoring seasons ever. Out of players who've averaged at least 19 shots per game in a season, Thomas ranks 7th in true shooting percentage all-time and 2nd all-time among guards – only trailing Stephen Curry's 2015-16 MVP season.

Using the interactive chart, you can compare Thomas' scoring to other top players in the league. To provide historical context to how masterful his season was, you can also compare against other historic scoring seasons.

The Off-Ball Evolution

Thomas' production generally comes off pick-and-rolls, isolations, and transitions, but this season, Thomas has evolved into an off-ball threat.

The Celtics motion offense utilizes faceup bigs that draw defenders to the perimeter; then through the use of screens and dribble handoffs, Thomas is afforded the space he needs to score effectively.

This video is a common variation of a set where Thomas starts in the right corner and runs through a screen. Simultaneously, a big at the top of the key dribbles towards Thomas for the handoff.

This forces the defender into a dilemma. If he goes under the screen, Thomas can pull up from 3. If he goes over, Thomas can attack the rim. If the defense denies lane entry, Thomas can kick it to an open man.

Aaaaand, checkmate.

What does the Future Hold

Against all odds, and all notions about height, Isaiah Thomas has become a star in the NBA. When describing why in the past he lacked respect from others in the league, Thomas stated "I'm not the typical point guard, and usually, people don't like what's not normal."

It's true. Thomas isn't normal. The Celtics aren't either: They're a winning team with more valuable assets than perhaps any other organization. How they play their hand over the following months and years will determine the heights the franchise and Thomas will reach together.
